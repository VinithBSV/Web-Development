MUSEUM OF CANDY

A donut is typically a ring-shaped pastry with a hole in the center, though variations can be filled or shaped differently. The classic ring donut has a soft, airy texture, achieved through a combination of yeast or baking powder, flour, sugar, and sometimes milk or eggs.Donuts are made by mixing a dough or batter, shaping it, and then frying it in hot oil. This process gives them their characteristic crispy exterior and tender interior.
